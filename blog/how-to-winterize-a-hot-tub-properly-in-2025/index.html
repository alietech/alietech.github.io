Why Winterize Your Hot Tub?

Winterizing your hot tub is essential if you live in an area with freezing conditions and do not plan to use it during the cold months. This process protects the hot tub components, such as the pipes, pump, and heater, from freezing and cracking, which can incur significant repair costs.

Step-by-Step Winterization Process

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, gather the necessary materials:

  • Garden hose
  • Wet/dry vacuum
  • Spa cleaning solution
  • Sponge or soft cloth
  • Funnel
  • Hot tub antifreeze (non-toxic)

Step 2: Power Down and Drain Your Hot Tub

  1. Turn off the Power: Disconnect the hot tub from the power source at the circuit breaker to ensure safety while you work.

  2. Drain the Hot Tub: Attach a garden hose to the drain valve and direct it away from the foundation of your house. Open the valve and let the water completely drain from the tub.

  3. Blow Out the Pipes: Use a wet/dry vacuum on the blow setting to remove any remaining water from the jets and plumbing lines. Repeat this process multiple times to ensure all water is evacuated.

Step 3: Clean the Hot Tub

  1. Remove and Clean the Filter: Take out the filter cartridge and clean it with a spa filter cleaner. Allow it to dry completely before storing it indoors throughout winter.

  2. Clean the Shell: Use a spa cleaning solution and a soft cloth or sponge to clean the shell of the hot tub thoroughly. Remove any residue or buildup.

Step 4: Add Antifreeze

  1. Protect the Plumbing: Pour non-toxic antifreeze into each jet, the filter inlet, and pump by using a funnel. This step is crucial to prevent any water remaining in these components from freezing and causing damage.

  2. Finish Off: Re-check each area to ensure complete antifreeze coverage, especially if your region experiences severe temperatures.

Step 5: Secure and Cover Your Hot Tub

  1. Seal the Lid: Ensure that the hot tub cover is clean and securely fastened to prevent debris or excess moisture from entering.

  2. Additional Protection: Consider wrapping the tub in a protective cover or tarp for extra insulation and security against harsh winter weather.
