## 1. **Inconsistent Texture**

One of the most common complaints about ice cream makers is the inconsistent texture of the frozen dessert. This issue is often caused by improper freezing of the bowl or an incorrect mixture ratio. In 2025, many ice cream aficionados are opting for models with built-in freezers to combat this problem. Additionally, ensuring that the mixture is pre-chilled can contribute to a smoother texture. ## 2. **Overfreezing**

Overfreezing is an issue where ice cream becomes too hard and difficult to scoop. This problem is often attributed to leaving the ice cream in the machine for too long or incorrect temperature settings. Many new models come with timers and auto-shutoff features to prevent this problem. Understanding how to balance churning time with storage is essential for maintaining the desired creamy texture. ## 4. **Leaking**

Leaking is another frequent complaint that often results from wear and tear. Gaskets might become loose or cracked over time, particularly if the machine is frequently used or improperly cleaned. Regular inspection and maintenance are key to preventing leaks. When in doubt, refer to user manuals or manufacturer guidelines for proper cleaning techniques to prolong the appliance's lifespan.
