As we approach 2025, the market for 60-inch TVs continues to be highly competitive. Innovations in screen technology, such as OLED and QLED panels, as well as increased production efficiencies, are likely to influence pricing structures. Based on current trends and market forecasts, here’s what you can expect:

  1. Entry-Level Models: These are designed for budget-conscious consumers who want a large screen experience without the bells and whistles. In 2025, entry-level 60-inch TVs are expected to be priced between $400 and $700. These models will likely include basic smart features and 4K resolution.

  2. Mid-Range Models: Offering a balance between price and performance, mid-range 60-inch TVs are predicted to range from $700 to $1,200. These sets may include enhanced picture quality with HDR support, more powerful processors, and additional smart functionalities.

  3. Premium Models: For those seeking the crème de la crème, premium 60-inch TVs will provide advanced features like OLED displays, superior sound systems, and AI-powered enhancements. These can range from $1,200 to over $2,500 in 2025, depending on the brand and specific features.

Several factors are anticipated to impact the pricing of 60-inch TVs in 2025:
