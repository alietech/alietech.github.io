Key Techniques for Improved Footwork

1. Basic Stance and Balance

Start by mastering the basic ready position. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, and weight balanced on the balls of your feet. This stance allows for quick lateral movements and provides a stable foundation for all types of shots.

2. The Side Shuffle

The side shuffle is essential for maintaining position during rallies. Practice moving quickly side-to-side without crossing your feet. This movement keeps your body aligned and ready to return shots from any angle.

3. Pivoting

Pivoting helps you turn swiftly without losing your balance. To practice pivoting, work on rotating your hips and shoulders smoothly while keeping your feet planted. This technique is especially useful when transitioning from forehand to backhand shots.

4. The One-Step Movement

Utilize the one-step movement to reach distant shots effectively. Practice extending one leg in the direction of the ball while keeping your opposite leg stable, helping you cover more ground with minimal effort.
