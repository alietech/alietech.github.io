OLED, or Organic Light-Emitting Diode, is a technology that uses organic compounds to emit light when electricity is applied. Unlike traditional LCD TVs that require backlighting, each pixel in an OLED display independently lights up and turns off. This feature allows OLED TVs to offer true blacks and vibrant colors, resulting in exceptional picture quality.
