Understanding the Ping Pong Serve Rules

1. The Ball Must Rest on an Open Palm

The serve begins with the ball resting freely on the open palm of the server’s stationary free hand. The reason for this rule is to prevent any spin being imparted on the ball before it is struck, ensuring fairness.

2. Tossing the Ball

The ball must be thrown vertically upwards at least 16 centimeters (about 6.3 inches) from the palm. The toss must be straight up, without imparting any spin, ensuring your opponent has a fair chance of seeing and reacting to the ball’s trajectory.

3. Striking The Ball

You must strike the ball behind the end line and above the level of the playing surface. The ball must first bounce on your side of the table and then over to your opponent’s side. This ensures that all serves start in an identical manner, preventing the server from gaining an unfair advantage.

4. Visibility

The ball must remain visible to the opponent throughout the serve. This means that you cannot let your body, arm, or any other object obstruct the view of your opponent. Using transparent shirts or standing further from the table can help achieve this.

5. Legal Contact Point

The point of contact with the ball must be behind the server’s end of the table. This rule helps maintain a standard distance from which the serve is made, across all places of play.

Improving Your Legal Serve

  • Practice Consistently: Regular practice under the guidance of a coach can ensure your technique adheres to the latest regulations.
  • Use Technology: Video recording your practice sessions can help you visually confirm if you’re meeting all legal serve requirements.
  • Seek Expertise: Consider joining table tennis workshops to stay updated with any rule changes and sharpen your skills with professional tips.
